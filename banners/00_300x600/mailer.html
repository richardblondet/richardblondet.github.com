WebMD:

Myths and Facts About Cancer

This content was selected by the WebMD Editorial staff and is solely under WebMD's editorial control.

MYTH:

Pain is a sure sign of cancer

Some cancers never cause pain. And while pain can be a part of life with some forms of cancer, doctors have many ways to manage it.

FACT:

There's an easy way to prevent cancer.

Don't smoke. If you do, quit. Worldwide, smoking causes 22% of all cancer deaths and about 71% of lung cancer deaths. It's the single biggest cause of cancer that you can just say no to.

MYTH:

Most cancer is inherited.

Cancer is caused by changes in your genes, but less than 10% of cancers are inherited. Most cancers are caused by aging and exposure to tobacco smoke and radiation.

FACT:

Cell phones don't cause cancer.

Changes in your genes cause cancer. The low-frequency energy your cell phone puts out doesn't affect your DNA.

MYTH:

Water bottles cause cancer.

You can ignore that chain email that says DEHA, a chemical in plastic, causes cancer. The Environmental Protection Agency and leading cancer researchers agree that it doesnt.

FACT:

Artificial sweeteners don't cause cancer.

There's no evidence that any artifical sweetener currently OK'd for sale in the U.S. causes cancer.
