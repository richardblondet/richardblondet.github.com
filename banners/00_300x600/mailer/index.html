WebMD Cold & Flu Relief:

When You Have High Blood Pressure

This content was selected by the WebMD Editorial staff and is solely under WebMD's editorial control.

Tip #1:

Talk Before You Take

Keep your blood pressure in check by talking to your pharmacist before you take medicines, prescription or nonprescription. Some may raise your blood pressure or not mix well with blood pressure drugs.

Tip #2:

Watch Decongestant Pills

They tighten blood vessels to stop swelling and stuffiness, but that can raise your BP. Stuffy? Ask your doctor about: a nasal decongestant spray, saline nasal spray, or other med that won't raise BP.

Tip #3:

Take a Warm Shower or Bath

Steam can help with stuffiness. Plus, it can keep your sinuses moist. A cup of hot tea will do the same trick.

Tip #4:

Target Your Symptoms

Looking at multi-symptoms cold medicine? Pick one that matches your symptoms. If you have high blood pressure, watch for the word "decongestant" on the label. If you see it, talk to your pharmacist.

Tip #5:

Spritz With Saline

Loosen congestion and keep your sinuses moist and comfortable with a saline nasal spray. To make your own: Stir 1/4 teaspoon of salt in 8 ounces of sterile water. Apply with a clean bulb syringe

Tip #6:

Gargle Away a Sore Throat

Ease sore throat pain the natural way -- gargle with salt watter. Mix a 1/2 teaspoon of salt with warm water. Gargle and then spit out.
