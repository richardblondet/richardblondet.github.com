Entry/Entry Video: During the period beginning at 12:00pm (noon) (PT), May 13, 2016, and ending at 11:59pm (PT) May 27, 2016 ("Entry Period"), a contestant ("Contestant") may enter this Contest by visiting www.myadventuresinbabysitting.com ("Website"), to potentially win a Prize (further defined in Section 8 below) by submitting a crazy, wacky or funny video (the "Entry Video"), in which you describe your most memorable babysitting experience (the "Theme") whether it was a babysitter the Contestant had or the Contestant was the babysitter. DO's & DON'Ts

Do's...

  • Do protect your privacy. Please don't mention any last names or other personal information in your Entry Video.
  • Do be original.

Entry Video has to be 100% original, so feel free to put your own spin on it.   Only submit a Entry Video that has not been submitted previously in any promotion or contest of any kind.

  • Have fun!

Don'ts...

  • Don't show any prominent brands or logos. That means no prominent and/or visible/recognizable use of brands (other than Disney-branded items) on clothes, sneakers, in the background, or anywhere. Avoid wearing clothes with visible logos.
  • Don't include anyone else in the Entry Video except yourself. You must be the only person in the Entry Video.
  • Don't use any music in your Entry Video.
  • Don't be rude or use profanity. Avoid bad language, rude gestures and other inappropriate stuff, and for your safety, don't try any risky moves or perform dangerous stunts.
  • Don't wear revealing clothing or display suggestive movements.
  • Don't submit a Entry Video that is too long or short in Duration, too big in File Size or in the wrong Format.
  • Don't use material you don't own.

No film or TV clips or other material that violates or infringes the rights of any person or entity (whether living or deceased).

Filmmaker Tips

  • Shoot in Landscape or Widescreen mode. Try to hold the camera so your Entry Video comes out wider than it is tall (like a movie screen), but this is a suggestion, not a requirement.
  • Be Bright. Use sunlight, light bulbs, whatever! Shoot with lots of light coming from behind the camera and shining on you, and try to wear bright clothing.
  • Speak Up. The closer you are to the camera or separate microphone, the better your voice will sound.
  • Keep it Steady.

Make sure your camera is on a tripod or make sure something is holding it steady.
